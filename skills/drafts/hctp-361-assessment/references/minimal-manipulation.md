# Minimal Manipulation Examples (21 CFR 1271.10(a)(1))

Source: "Regulatory Considerations for Human Cells, Tissues, and Cellular and Tissue-Based Products: Minimal Manipulation and Homologous Use" (July 2020)

## Definitions

**Structural tissue** — processing does not alter the **original relevant characteristics** relating to utility for reconstruction, repair, or replacement (21 CFR 1271.3(f)(1))

**Cells/nonstructural tissue** — processing does not alter the **relevant biological characteristics** of cells or tissues (21 CFR 1271.3(f)(2))

If information does not exist to show processing meets minimal manipulation, FDA considers it **more than minimal manipulation**.

## Step 1: Classify the HCT/P

Classification is based on the HCT/P's characteristics **in the donor**, before recovery and before any processing.

### Structural Tissues
Physically support, serve as barrier/conduit, connect, cover, or cushion:
- Bone
- Skin
- Amniotic membrane and umbilical cord
- Blood vessel
- Adipose tissue
- Articular cartilage
- Non-articular cartilage
- Tendon or ligament

### Cells/Nonstructural Tissues
Serve metabolic or other biochemical roles (hematopoietic, immune, endocrine):
- Reproductive cells/tissues (oocytes)
- Hematopoietic stem/progenitor cells (cord blood)
- Lymph nodes and thymus
- Parathyroid glands
- Peripheral nerve
- Pancreatic tissue

Note: Secreted body fluids (e.g., amniotic fluid) are generally NOT HCT/Ps. Cells from secreted body fluids ARE HCT/Ps — the cells/nonstructural definition applies.

## Step 2: Evaluate All Processing Steps

Consider **all** processing steps. Processing includes cutting, grinding, shaping, culturing, enzymatic digestion, and decellularization (21 CFR 1271.3(ff)).

## Structural Tissue Examples

### Bone

Original relevant characteristics: strength, resistance to compression (utility: support body, protect internal structures)

| Processing | Determination | Reasoning |
|-----------|--------------|-----------|
| Milling, grinding, shaping into chips/particles | Minimal | Does not alter strength/compression properties |
| Threading/machining into dowels, screws, pins | Minimal | Does not alter structural characteristics |
| Acid + elevated temp to demineralize and form gel | More than minimal | Alters structural support characteristics |

### Amniotic Membrane

Original relevant characteristics: physical integrity, tensile strength, elasticity (utility: barrier)

| Processing | Determination | Reasoning |
|-----------|--------------|-----------|
| Preserve and package in sheets | Minimal | Barrier characteristics preserved |
| Grind and lyophilize into particles | More than minimal | Barrier function destroyed |
| Remove chorion and other cells | Minimal | Barrier characteristics preserved |

### Skin

Original relevant characteristics: large surface area, keratinized water-resistant epidermis, dense/strong/flexible dermis (utility: protective covering)

| Processing | Determination | Reasoning |
|-----------|--------------|-----------|
| Mechanical meshing + cryopreservation, packaged as sheets | Minimal | Covering characteristics preserved |
| Remove epidermis, grind dermis into particles | More than minimal | Covering characteristics destroyed |
| Remove epidermis, freeze-dry remaining dermis (acellular dermal matrix) | Minimal | Covering characteristics preserved |

### Fascia Lata

Original relevant characteristics: strength, flexibility, fibrous sheet-like configuration (utility: cover muscle, aid movement)

| Processing | Determination | Reasoning |
|-----------|--------------|-----------|
| Grind sheets into particles | More than minimal | Sheet configuration destroyed |

### Adipose Tissue

Original relevant characteristics: bulk, lipid storage capacity (utility: cushioning and support)

| Processing | Determination | Reasoning |
|-----------|--------------|-----------|
| Centrifuge lipoaspirate to remove debris only | Minimal | Bulk and lipid storage preserved |
| Decellularize (remove cells, leave ECM) | More than minimal | Bulk and lipid storage altered |
| Enzymatic digestion/mechanical disruption to isolate SVF | More than minimal | Structural components eliminated |

**Note on adipose:** Even when cells are isolated from adipose tissue (structural), the **structural tissue** definition of minimal manipulation applies, because classification is based on the HCT/P as it exists in the donor.

### Cartilage

Original relevant characteristics: firmness, smoothness, flexibility, resistance to deformation (utility: load-bearing)

| Processing | Determination | Reasoning |
|-----------|--------------|-----------|
| Homogenize into slurry | More than minimal | Load-bearing characteristics destroyed |

### Ligament

Original relevant characteristics: tensile strength from bundled fibrous collagen (utility: attach bone to bone, aid movement/stability)

| Processing | Determination | Reasoning |
|-----------|--------------|-----------|
| Disaggregate collagen fibers | More than minimal | Tensile strength destroyed |

## Cells/Nonstructural Tissue Examples

### Hematopoietic Stem/Progenitor Cells (HPCs)

Relevant biological characteristics: ability to repopulate bone marrow by self-renewal and differentiating along myeloid and lymphoid cell lines

| Processing | Determination | Reasoning |
|-----------|--------------|-----------|
| Cell selection on mobilized peripheral blood apheresis | Minimal | Self-renewal and differentiation unaltered |
| Culture under specific conditions to produce terminally differentiated cells | More than minimal | Multipotency and self-renewal altered |
| Cell selection + incubation with growth factors for expansion (cord blood) | More than minimal | Cell-surface proteins, lineage markers, activation state altered |

## Storage

Storage that does not alter original relevant characteristics is generally minimal manipulation:
- Tissue medium + refrigeration (buffer solution)
- Cryopreservation in liquid nitrogen vapor

## General Principles

1. Classification (structural vs. cellular) is determined from the **donor's** perspective, before recovery
2. **All** processing steps must be considered cumulatively
3. When an HCT/P has both structural and cellular functions, it is classified as one or the other (not both)
4. Structural tissues: focus on whether physical/mechanical properties are preserved
5. Cells/nonstructural tissues: focus on whether biological characteristics (differentiation state, proliferation potential, metabolic activity) are preserved
6. Absence of evidence = more than minimal manipulation
