# Same Surgical Procedure Exception (21 CFR 1271.15(b))

Source: "Same Surgical Procedure Exception under 21 CFR 1271.15(b): Questions and Answers Regarding the Scope of the Exception; Guidance for Industry" (November 2017)

## Regulatory Text

> "You are not required to comply with the requirements of this Part if you are an establishment that removes HCT/Ps from an individual and implants such HCT/Ps into the same individual during the same surgical procedure." — 21 CFR 1271.15(b)

## Q&A Summary

### Q1: Relationship to the Four Criteria (1271.10(a))

The SSP exception is **independent** from the four criteria in 1271.10(a). It is assessed **before** considering the four criteria. If the exception applies, the establishment need not consider the four criteria at all.

- Minimal manipulation (1271.10(a)(1)) and homologous use (1271.10(a)(2)) are NOT considered when determining SSP eligibility.

### Q2: When Does the Exception Apply?

All three conditions must be met:

1. **Autologous use** — Remove and implant into the same individual
2. **Same surgical procedure** — Within a single operation at the same establishment
3. **"Such HCT/P"** — The HCT/P remains in its original form

### Q3: What Is "Such HCT/P"?

An HCT/P remains "such HCT/P" when it is in its **original form**. Generally, only these processing steps preserve original form:
- Rinsing
- Cleansing
- Sizing
- Shaping

**Critical distinction:** "Such HCT/P" (1271.15(b)) is a **different and narrower standard** than "minimal manipulation" (1271.10(a)(1)). Processing that qualifies as minimal manipulation may still disqualify from the SSP exception.

### Q4: Autologous Use

Defined in 21 CFR 1271.3(a): implantation, transplantation, infusion, or transfer of human cells or tissue back into the individual from whom they were recovered.

### Q5: Same Surgical Procedure Definition

Procedures involving an incision or instrumentation during which an HCT/P is removed from and implanted into the same individual within a **single operation** at the **same establishment**.

Examples: autologous skin grafting, coronary artery bypass with autologous vein/artery grafting.

### Q6: Multi-Operation Exceptions

Generally, procedures with more than a single operation do NOT qualify. However, two **limited exceptions** exist:

1. **Craniotomy/craniectomy** — with subsequent reimplantation of bone flap to reverse cranial defect
2. **Parathyroidectomy** — with subsequent reimplantation of tissue portion to preserve parathyroid function

During the interval between operations:
- HCT/P may be rinsed, cleansed, and temporarily stored after labeling
- No other processing or manufacturing steps beyond labeling and storage
- For cranial bone flaps/parathyroid tissue, temporary storage may include disinfecting or cryopreserving

**Same-establishment requirement:** The exception generally applies only when both removal and implantation occur at the same establishment. Shipping to another establishment = distribution = manufacturing, which requires registration, listing, and Part 1271 compliance.

**Medical necessity exception for craniotomy/parathyroidectomy:** If medical necessity requires sending the HCT/P to a different establishment for reimplantation, FDA does not intend to object, provided precautions protect against contamination and cross-contamination.

### Q7: Processing Before Reimplantation

Generally, an establishment that processes an autologous HCT/P prior to implantation does NOT qualify for the exception.

**Processing that preserves "such HCT/P" status:**
- Rinsing and cleansing
- Centrifugation or filtration **solely to remove debris** (lipids, blood, bone particles)
- Sizing and shaping (e.g., dilatation of vascular graft, cutting parathyroid tissue, meshing skin grafts)

**Processing that disqualifies:**
- Centrifugation or filtration for cell isolation
- Cell expansion
- Cell activation
- Enzymatic digestion
- Mechanical disruption to isolate cellular components

## Adipose Tissue Examples

**Example 7-1 (SSP applies):** Adipose tissue recovered by tumescent liposuction. Lipoaspirate centrifuged to remove debris and extracellular fluid. No steps to isolate stem cells/SVF. Adipose tissue retains original form as connective tissue of adipocyte clusters with reticular fiber network and small blood vessels. Reimplanted into same patient. **Qualifies for exception.**

**Example 7-2 (SSP does NOT apply):** Adipose tissue recovered by tumescent liposuction, then processed (enzymatic digestion, mechanical disruption) to isolate stromal vascular fraction (SVF). Cell isolation causes adipose tissue to no longer be "such HCT/P." **Does not qualify.**

## Key Definitions

- **Establishment:** Place of business under one management, at one general physical location, that engages in HCT/P manufacture (21 CFR 1271.3(b))
- **Processing:** Any activity on an HCT/P other than recovery, donor screening, donor testing, storage, labeling, packaging, or distribution (21 CFR 1271.3(ff))
- **Distribution:** Any conveyance or shipment of an HCT/P that has been determined to meet all release criteria (21 CFR 1271.3(bb))
- **Manufacture:** Any or all steps in recovery, processing, storage, labeling, packaging, or distribution of any human cell or tissue, and screening/testing of the donor (21 CFR 1271.3(e))

## PRP Note

Platelet rich plasma (PRP) and other blood products are **not** HCT/Ps. The SSP exception in 1271.15(b) is not applicable to PRP.
