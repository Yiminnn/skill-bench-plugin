# Homologous Use Examples (21 CFR 1271.10(a)(2))

Source: "Regulatory Considerations for Human Cells, Tissues, and Cellular and Tissue-Based Products: Minimal Manipulation and Homologous Use" (July 2020)

## Definition

> Homologous use means the repair, reconstruction, replacement, or supplementation of a recipient's cells or tissues with an HCT/P that performs the **same basic function or functions** in the recipient as in the donor. — 21 CFR 1271.3(c)

## Key Principles

1. **Basic function** = what the HCT/P does biologically/physiologically in its native state in the donor
2. **Does NOT need to be in the same anatomic location** — different location is acceptable if function is the same
3. The HCT/P need not perform ALL donor functions in the recipient — but any function it IS expected to perform must be a basic function from the donor
4. Basic functions are **well understood** — no lab/clinical studies needed to identify them
5. Clinical effects in the recipient that are NOT basic functions of the HCT/P in the donor are NOT considered homologous use
6. **Intended use** is determined by labeling, advertising, and other indications of manufacturer's objective intent
7. If intended for a **"myriad of diseases or conditions"** — likely NOT homologous use

## Definitions of Repair/Reconstruction/Replacement/Supplementation

- **Repair:** physical or mechanical restoration of tissues, including covering or protecting
- **Reconstruction:** surgical reassembling or re-forming
- **Replacement:** substitution of a missing tissue or cell
- **Supplementation:** to add to or complete

These are not mutually exclusive — an HCT/P can perform more than one for a given use.

## Homologous Use Examples by Tissue Type

### Heart Valve
**Basic function:** ensuring unidirectional blood flow within the heart

| Use | Homologous? | Reasoning |
|-----|------------|-----------|
| Replace dysfunctional heart valve | Yes | Same function: unidirectional blood flow |

### Pericardium
**Basic functions:** covering, protecting against infection, fixing heart to mediastinum, providing lubrication for heart movement

| Use | Homologous? | Reasoning |
|-----|------------|-----------|
| Wound covering for dura mater defects | Yes | Covering is a basic function |
| Replace a dysfunctional heart valve | No | Facilitating unidirectional blood flow is NOT a basic pericardium function |

### Cornea
**Basic functions:** protecting the eye, serving as outermost lens

| Use | Homologous? | Reasoning |
|-----|------------|-----------|
| Corneal graft for corneal blindness | Yes | Same protective and lens functions |

### Blood Vessel (Vein/Artery)
**Basic function:** conduit for blood flow

| Use | Homologous? | Reasoning |
|-----|------------|-----------|
| Arteriovenous access during hemodialysis | Yes | Conduit for blood flow (supplementation) |

### Amniotic Membrane
**Basic functions:** selective barrier for nutrient movement, protecting fetus from maternal environment, covering to enclose fetus and retain fluid

| Use | Homologous? | Reasoning |
|-----|------------|-----------|
| Bone tissue replacement/bone regeneration | No | Bone regeneration is NOT a basic function |
| Wound healing/reducing scarring and inflammation | No | Anti-scarring/anti-inflammatory effects are clinical effects in recipient, not basic donor functions |
| Ocular surface covering for protection | Yes | Covering and protection are basic functions |

### Adipose Tissue
**Basic functions:** cushioning and support for tissues/organs, storing energy as lipids, insulating the body

| Use | Homologous? | Reasoning |
|-----|------------|-----------|
| Fill voids in face/hands (cosmetic) | Yes | Cushioning and support |
| Breast reconstruction/augmentation (subcutaneous) | Yes | Cushioning and support |
| Treat musculoskeletal conditions (arthritis, tendonitis) by regenerating cartilage/tendon | No | Regeneration of cartilage/tendon is NOT a basic function |
| Treat neurological disorders (e.g., MS) via immunomodulation/remyelinization | No | Immunomodulation/remyelinization are NOT basic functions |
| Treat degenerative, inflammatory, or demyelinating disorders | No | Not basic functions of adipose tissue |

### Skin/Dermis
**Basic functions:** covering, protecting body from external force, serving as water-resistant barrier; dermis provides support and protection from mechanical stress

| Use | Homologous? | Reasoning |
|-----|------------|-----------|
| Skin graft to cover a burn wound | Yes | Covering and protection |
| Acellular dermal product for tendon support/protection/reinforcement/covering | Yes | Support and protection from mechanical stress (same function, different location) |
| Acellular dermal product for tendon replacement/repair | No | Connection between muscle and bone is NOT a basic dermis function |
| Dermal matrix for filling facial wrinkles | Yes | Supplementation — support function |

### Bone
**Basic functions:** supporting the body, protecting internal structures

| Use | Homologous? | Reasoning |
|-----|------------|-----------|
| Supplement bony voids/gaps in extremities, cranium, spinal column | Yes | Supporting body/protecting structures |
| Augmentation for posterior lateral spinal fusions | Yes | Supporting body |
| Bone chips to supplement bony defects | Yes | Supplementation of support function |

### Hematopoietic Stem/Progenitor Cells (HPCs)
**Basic functions:** forming and replenishing the lymphohematopoietic system

| Use | Homologous? | Reasoning |
|-----|------------|-----------|
| Transplant for inherited/acquired hematopoietic disorder or post-myeloablative treatment | Yes | Reconstituting hematopoietic system |
| Infusion into artery for ventricular remodeling post-MI | No | Limiting ventricular remodeling is NOT a basic bone marrow function |
| IV infusion for cerebral palsy (neuronal repair via paracrine signaling) | No | Neuronal repair is NOT a basic function; insufficient evidence |

### Pancreatic Islets
**Basic function:** regulating glucose homeostasis

| Use | Homologous? | Reasoning |
|-----|------------|-----------|
| Transplant into liver via portal vein for endocrine function post-pancreatectomy | Yes | Glucose homeostasis (different location, same function) |

## Manufacturer's Objective Intent

Determined by:
- Labeling claims
- Advertising matter
- Oral or written statements by manufacturer or representatives
- Circumstances surrounding distribution

An HCT/P meets the homologous use criterion when its labeling, advertising, and other indications refer to **only** homologous uses. Any reference to non-homologous uses means the criterion is NOT met.
